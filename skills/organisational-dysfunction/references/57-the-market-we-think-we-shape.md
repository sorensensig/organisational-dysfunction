# The market we think we shape

*Dysfunction `#57` in Trond Hjorteland's ["Organisational Dysfunction of the Day"](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/trondhjort_opensystemstheory-sociotechnical-orgdesign-activity-7476153267648057344-Dpqa) series — synthesised through open sociotechnical systems theory; paraphrased, not quoted.*

## How it shows up

- You have an advisory board, conference slots, and industry working groups — all the trappings of shaping your market — yet they mostly review what's already been decided.
- The advisory board is shown pre-baked features; the conference talk describes the product you already built rather than the need it should meet.
- Working-group documents get written and then nothing is implemented.
- Internally, decisions are actually driven by competitive analysis, quarterly OKRs, and whatever sales last heard. You are present in your environment, but passive in it.

## The sociotechnical diagnosis

An open system survives by maintaining an *active* relationship with its environment — not just absorbing signals from it but acting on it. This dysfunction is responsiveness without shaping: the organisation is brilliant at scanning and reacting, and systematically underinvests in actually molding the field it operates in. It treats the environment as something to read at a safe distance rather than something to change.

The tell is in the instruments. Competitive analysis, OKRs, the sales feedback loop, the advisory board reviewing finished work — these are all *problem-solving* instruments: internal, reactive, aimed at getting a predefined answer right. None of them is *puzzle-solving*: collaborative, exploratory, working with customers and partners on a future none of you can yet specify. In a turbulent environment, problem-solving alone is not enough, because the relevant uncertainty isn't "what's the answer" but "what's the question we're all facing together."

So the shaping is theatre. The forums exist, but they're wired as one-way broadcast and after-the-fact validation, not as joint inquiry. The organisation mistakes activity in the environment for influence over it.

## What to do

**The real fix is structural — treat the future as a puzzle to be explored together, not a problem to be solved internally.**
- Build genuinely bidirectional forums: bring customers, partners, and the wider field into shaping conditions, not into reviewing decisions already made.
- Redirect the advisory board and working groups from validating finished work to co-exploring problems that are still open.
- Aim at conditions where the whole system — customers, partners, you — can develop sustainably, rather than optimising only your own position within it.

**If you can't change the structure yet:**
- Distinguish your problem-solving instruments from your puzzle-solving ones, and notice you almost certainly have none of the latter.
- Convert at least one existing forum from broadcast to joint inquiry — go in with a real open question, not a finished slide.
- Reframe "we engage with the market" honestly as "we describe it from a distance", so the gap becomes a thing to fix rather than a thing to congratulate yourselves on.
